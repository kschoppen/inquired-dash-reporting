Status Quo
🧩
Districts cobbling programs together / Workshop-style curriculum
The largest "competitor" by volume. Many K–5 ELA decisions default to patchwork — existing basal + supplemental phonics kit + teacher-created read-alouds. Workshop-model districts (Lucy Calkins legacy) are actively replacing but often land on a known brand rather than something new. This is the inertia Inkwell has to overcome, not just a named competitor. Key wedge: science of reading mandates and state legislative pressure on districts to move off unaligned programs.
